# Design Context

## Users

Designers, developers, and creative technologists browsing the portfolio. They arrive to evaluate craft quality, explore interactive demos, and understand the author's design sensibility. The context is unhurried — they're browsing, not completing a task.

## Brand Personality

**Calm, Intentional, Craft-focused.** Every detail serves a purpose. The site speaks through its work, not through decoration. Quiet confidence over loud expression.

## Aesthetic Direction

- **Visual tone:** Minimalist editorial — content-first, strong typographic hierarchy, generous negative space
- **References:** Sites curated on Dead Simple Sites — stripped-back, purposeful design with nothing extraneous
- **Anti-references:** Generic SaaS dashboards, template-driven portfolios, heavy gradient/glassmorphism aesthetics
- **Theme:** Dark-mode exclusive. Warm dark neutrals (`#141312` base) with a single teal accent (`#a6cdd2`)
- **Typography:** Serif (Fraunces) for titles and hierarchy, sans (Raleway) for body, mono (DM Mono) for metadata and timestamps
- **Motion:** Spring-based animations for organic, natural feel. Understated transitions — motion serves function, never decoration

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't decorate** — Let the interactive work speak. The site's job is to frame it, not compete with it.
2. **Quiet over loud** — Prefer subtle hover states, gentle fades, and understated transitions. Avoid anything flashy or attention-grabbing in the chrome.
3. **Typography is the design** — Use type weight, size, and color (not borders, backgrounds, or ornament) to create hierarchy.
4. **Every pixel intentional** — No placeholder content, no default styles left unquestioned. If it's there, it earns its place.
5. **Craft invites exploration** — Polished details and smooth interactions make people want to click around and discover more.
